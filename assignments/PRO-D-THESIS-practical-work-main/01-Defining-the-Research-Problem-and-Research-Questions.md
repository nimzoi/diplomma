# Assignment 1 — Defining the Research Problem and Research Questions

## 1. Objective of the Assignment

The purpose of this assignment is to formally define the intellectual foundation of the thesis.

At this stage, the student is expected to demonstrate:

* The ability to identify a technically meaningful problem
* Awareness of its practical and/or scientific relevance
* Understanding of measurability and evaluability
* Initial research maturity appropriate for an engineering thesis

This assignment establishes the conceptual backbone for all subsequent work (literature review, experimental design, implementation, and evaluation).

## 2. Scope of Work to Be Delivered

The student must submit a structured document (2–4 pages) containing the following elements:

### A. Problem Context and Motivation

Provide a concise but academically grounded description of:

* The broader domain (e.g., medical diagnostics, NLP for low-resource languages, anomaly detection in IoT systems)
* The practical or scientific gap being addressed
* Why the problem is non-trivial

**Expectations:**

* The problem must go beyond "implementation of algorithm X."
* The motivation must be rooted in real constraints (data scarcity, computational limits, interpretability requirements, domain-specific challenges, etc.).
* Avoid generic claims ("AI is important nowadays").
* The problem should be positioned as a response to a real limitation, inefficiency, or open question.

### B. Formal Research Problem Statement

Provide a clear and precise formulation of the problem in 1–3 logically structured statements.

The statement should:

* Define the input space (data type, scale, assumptions)
* Define the expected output
* Implicitly or explicitly indicate constraints

Example structure (abstract form): *The problem consists of designing and evaluating a predictive model that maps X to Y under constraints Z, with the goal of optimizing metric M.*

The formulation must be:

* Technically unambiguous
* Measurable
* Feasible within one semester of engineering implementation

### C. Research Question

The research question must:

* Be answerable through empirical experimentation
* Imply comparison, evaluation, or validation
* Be aligned with the defined problem

Examples of acceptable forms:

* Does method A outperform baseline B on dataset D under metric M?
* To what extent does feature engineering strategy S improve predictive stability?
* How does model complexity influence generalization performance under limited data conditions?

The question must avoid:

* Purely descriptive objectives
* Implementation-only goals
* Overly broad theoretical inquiries

### D. Hypothesis (If Applicable)

Where appropriate, formulate a testable hypothesis.

The hypothesis should:

* Predict a measurable outcome
* Be falsifiable
* Be directly connected to evaluation metrics

Example structure: *It is hypothesized that incorporating domain-specific feature engineering will increase F1-score by at least 5% compared to a logistic regression baseline.*

If a hypothesis is not appropriate (e.g., exploratory benchmarking study), this must be justified.

### E. Success Criteria and Evaluation Framework

Define:

* Primary evaluation metric(s)
* Baseline reference point
* Quantitative threshold for improvement

**Expectations:**

* Metrics must match the nature of the problem (classification, regression, ranking, generation, etc.).
* The student must justify metric choice.
* The success criteria must be realistic and measurable.

This section ensures that the project will later support rigorous experimental validation.

### F. Feasibility and Scope Justification

Provide a brief but critical reflection addressing:

* Data availability
* Computational constraints
* Required technical competencies
* Project risks

An engineering thesis must demonstrate realistic planning. Overly ambitious or vaguely defined projects will be reformulated.

## 3. Academic Expectations for an Engineering Thesis

At the engineering level, the thesis must:

* Demonstrate systematic problem formulation
* Include comparative experimentation
* Provide measurable evaluation
* Show technical autonomy and structured thinking
* Reflect awareness of limitations

It is not expected to:

* Propose a novel algorithm at the research frontier
* Achieve state-of-the-art performance globally
* Produce publishable theoretical contributions

However, it must clearly demonstrate engineering reasoning and methodological rigor.

## 4. Quality Evaluation Criteria

Assignment 1 will be evaluated according to:

1. Clarity and precision of formulation
2. Measurability of objectives
3. Logical coherence between problem, question, and evaluation
4. Realistic scope definition
5. Academic tone and structure

Only after approval of this assignment may the student proceed to the systematic literature review (Assignment 2).

## 5. Position Within the Seminar Continuum

This assignment defines the intellectual anchor of the thesis.

* Assignment 2 will verify the problem against existing literature.
* Assignment 3 will translate the problem into experimental architecture.
* All modeling and implementation decisions will derive from this formulation.

A weak problem definition inevitably leads to a weak thesis. Therefore, this stage is foundational and non-negotiable.
