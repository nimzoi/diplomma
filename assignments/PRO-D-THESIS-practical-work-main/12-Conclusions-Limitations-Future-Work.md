# Assignment 12 — Conclusions, Limitations, Future Work

## 1. Objective of the Assignment

The purpose of this assignment is to formally conclude the practical component of the thesis by synthesizing findings, articulating limitations, and defining realistic directions for future development.

At this stage, the student must demonstrate:

* Ability to draw evidence-based conclusions
* Intellectual honesty regarding limitations
* Awareness of broader implications of the work
* Readiness for final refinement in Semester III

This assignment is not a repetition of results. It is a structured scientific synthesis that closes the research loop initiated in Assignment 1.

## 2. Scope of Work to Be Delivered

The student must submit:

* A draft of the Conclusion chapter
* A structured Limitations subsection
* A Future Work subsection
* A final checklist of remaining improvements for Semester III

The document must contain the following components.

### A. Direct Answer to the Research Question

This section must explicitly restate:

* The research question
* The hypothesis (if formulated)

Then provide:

* A concise answer supported by empirical evidence
* A summary of the most important results
* Quantitative confirmation where relevant

The student must clearly state:

* Whether the hypothesis was supported or rejected
* Under what conditions the conclusions hold

The answer must be proportional to the evidence. Overgeneralization beyond the dataset or experimental conditions is unacceptable.

### B. Summary of Technical Contribution

Provide a structured summary of:

* What was implemented
* What was compared
* What methodological discipline was applied

Emphasize:

* Engineering rigor
* Controlled comparison
* Reproducibility

This section should briefly clarify the thesis contribution without repeating detailed results.

### C. Limitations of the Study

This section is mandatory and critical.

The student must identify:

* Dataset limitations (size, bias, representativeness)
* Model limitations (overfitting risk, interpretability constraints, computational cost)
* Evaluation limitations (metric selection, experimental scope)
* External validity constraints

Each limitation must include:

* Explanation of its potential impact
* Clarification of whether it affects conclusions

A mature engineering thesis acknowledges constraints openly. Failure to discuss limitations weakens scientific credibility.

### D. Future Work Directions

Future work must be:

* Realistic
* Logically derived from identified limitations
* Technically grounded

Possible directions may include:

* Testing on larger or different datasets
* Alternative architectures
* Improved interpretability methods
* Deployment or optimization improvements

Future work must not introduce entirely new research problems unrelated to the thesis scope. The purpose is to show awareness of development pathways, not speculative expansion.

### E. Final Coherence Verification

Conclude the document with a brief structured checklist confirming:

* The research question is answered
* All experiments are documented
* All results are reproducible
* The repository is finalized
* Writing inconsistencies are identified for correction in Semester III

This ensures readiness for transition to final editing and defense preparation.

## 3. Academic Expectations for an Engineering Thesis

At the engineering level, the final chapter must demonstrate:

* Structured synthesis
* Evidence-based reasoning
* Balanced evaluation
* Technical maturity

The thesis is expected to:

* Provide a clear and honest conclusion
* Demonstrate engineering discipline
* Maintain proportional claims

It must avoid:

* Repetition of results without synthesis
* Inflated claims of novelty
* Ignoring methodological constraints

A strong conclusion reflects clarity of thought, not length.

## 4. Quality Evaluation Criteria

Assignment 12 will be evaluated based on:

1. Clarity and precision of conclusions
2. Logical consistency with experimental evidence
3. Depth and honesty of limitations
4. Realism of future work
5. Overall scientific maturity

Approval of this assignment confirms that:

* The practical component of the thesis is complete.
* The project is ready for final editorial refinement.
* The student is prepared to enter the final semester.

## 5. Position Within the Seminar Continuum

* Assignment 1 defined the research problem.
* Assignments 2–3 validated and structured it.
* Assignments 4–8 implemented it.
* Assignment 9 synthesized results.
* Assignments 10–11 formalized and verified coherence.
* Assignment 12 closes the scientific argument.

At this stage:

* No new experimental work should be introduced.
* The thesis must function as a complete and defensible academic document.
* Semester III will focus on editorial refinement, formatting, and defense preparation.

This concludes the full structured seminar sequence for Semester II.
