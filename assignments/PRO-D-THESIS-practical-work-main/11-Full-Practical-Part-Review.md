# Assignment 11 — Full Practical Part Review

## 1. Objective of the Assignment

The purpose of this assignment is to critically assess the practical component of the thesis as a unified scientific work.

At this stage, the student must demonstrate:

* Ability to evaluate their own work structurally and critically
* Logical consistency between research question, methodology, and results
* Awareness of missing elements or weak justifications
* Readiness for final consolidation

This is not a stage of adding new experiments unless strictly necessary. It is a stage of verification, refinement, and strengthening of scientific coherence. The objective is to ensure that the thesis does not consist of strong individual sections that lack logical integration.

## 2. Scope of Work to Be Delivered

The student must submit:

* The full draft of the practical component (integrated chapters)
* Repository access (final experimental state)
* A short self-assessment report (2–3 pages) identifying strengths and weaknesses

The submission must enable a full review of:

* Methodology
* Implementation
* Results
* Interpretation

The document must contain the following structured reflection.

### A. Logical Continuity Verification

The student must explicitly verify and describe:

* Whether the research question defined in Assignment 1 is directly addressed in the results section
* Whether the identified research gap (Assignment 2) is meaningfully engaged
* Whether the experimental design (Assignment 3) was fully implemented

This section must answer:

* Does every experiment serve the research objective?
* Are there experiments that do not contribute to the central question?

Scientific coherence is mandatory.

### B. Completeness of Experimental Evidence

Evaluate whether:

* Baselines are properly implemented and compared
* The primary model evaluation is sufficient
* Error analysis and interpretability are adequately developed
* Results are reproducible

The student must identify:

* Any weak comparisons
* Any missing justifications
* Any inconsistencies between reported metrics and conclusions

This is a self-critical diagnostic stage.

### C. Internal Consistency of Writing

Assess:

* Terminology consistency
* Alignment between tables, figures, and text
* Clarity of methodological descriptions
* Redundancy or structural fragmentation

The thesis must read as a unified argument, not as separate reports combined. This includes checking:

* Whether figures are properly referenced
* Whether all claims are supported by evidence
* Whether interpretation follows logically from results

### D. Technical Integrity and Reproducibility Check

Confirm that:

* The final repository reflects the reported results
* Configuration files correspond to described experiments
* Random seeds and splits are consistent
* There are no undocumented manual adjustments

The student must verify that:

* Re-running the pipeline produces consistent results
* No "temporary" code remains in the repository

This is the final engineering integrity verification.

### E. Identification of Necessary Corrections

The student must prepare a short structured list including:

* Required corrections before submission
* Optional improvements
* Non-critical refinements

This list must prioritize:

1. Scientific validity
2. Logical coherence
3. Clarity
4. Formatting issues (last priority)

## 3. Academic Expectations for an Engineering Thesis

At the engineering level, this stage must demonstrate:

* Intellectual honesty
* Structural thinking
* Critical self-evaluation
* Maturity in assessing one's own methodological rigor

The thesis must show:

* Internal logical integrity
* Justified conclusions
* Transparent limitations

It must avoid:

* Inconsistent claims
* Selective interpretation
* Structural fragmentation

A technically correct thesis can still be weak if it lacks coherence.

## 4. Quality Evaluation Criteria

Assignment 11 will be evaluated based on:

1. Structural coherence of the practical component
2. Alignment between research question and conclusions
3. Completeness of experimental validation
4. Clarity and consistency of writing
5. Engineering integrity and reproducibility

Approval of this assignment confirms readiness for finalization and conclusion writing.

## 5. Position Within the Seminar Continuum

* Assignment 10 initiated formal writing.
* Assignment 11 verifies scientific and structural completeness.
* Assignment 12 will finalize conclusions, limitations, future work, and preparation for submission and defense.

At this stage:

* Major methodological changes are discouraged.
* Only corrections that strengthen coherence or validity are allowed.
* The thesis must now function as a complete academic document.

The project is no longer being built — it is being critically refined.
