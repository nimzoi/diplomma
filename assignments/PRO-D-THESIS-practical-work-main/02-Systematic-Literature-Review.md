# Assignment 2 — Systematic Literature Review

## 1. Objective of the Assignment

The purpose of this assignment is to position the previously defined research problem within the existing body of knowledge.

At this stage, the student must demonstrate:

* Ability to search and select relevant scientific sources
* Capacity for analytical comparison rather than descriptive summary
* Understanding of methodological trends in the domain
* Awareness of current limitations and research gaps

This assignment validates whether the research problem defined in Assignment 1 is:

* Relevant
* Non-trivial
* Not already solved in a straightforward manner

The literature review serves as the theoretical and methodological foundation for the experimental design in Assignment 3.

## 2. Scope of Work to Be Delivered

The student must submit:

* A structured document (4–8 pages)
* A comparative synthesis table
* A preliminary bibliography formatted according to academic standards

The work must contain the following components:

### A. Search Strategy and Source Selection

Provide a brief methodological description of:

* Databases used (e.g., IEEE Xplore, ACM Digital Library, Springer, arXiv)
* Keywords and search queries
* Inclusion/exclusion criteria
* Time range considered

**Minimum scope:**

* 5 peer-reviewed scientific papers
* 3–5 high-quality industry or benchmark reports
* Additional relevant sources where justified

The selection must prioritize methodological relevance over superficial topical similarity. The review must not rely on blogs, tutorials, or non-scientific sources as primary references.

### B. Thematic Organization of Literature

The review must be structured logically, not chronologically.

Possible structuring principles:

* By methodological family (e.g., classical ML vs. deep learning approaches)
* By problem formulation variants
* By dataset characteristics
* By evaluation methodology

Each subsection must:

* Identify common methodological patterns
* Highlight differences in experimental design
* Discuss evaluation metrics used

The student is expected to synthesize — not summarize sequentially.

### C. Comparative Analytical Table

Prepare a structured comparison table including at minimum:

* Authors / Year
* Problem formulation
* Dataset(s)
* Methods applied
* Evaluation metrics
* Key results
* Identified limitations

This table must support analytical discussion in the text. Its purpose is to enable structured reasoning about methodological trends and research gaps.

### D. Critical Analysis and Research Gap Identification

This section is central to the assignment.

The student must:

* Identify limitations across studies (e.g., small datasets, lack of reproducibility, absence of baseline comparisons, limited interpretability, overfitting risks)
* Compare experimental setups
* Assess robustness and generalizability of reported results

Then explicitly answer:

1. What remains insufficiently addressed?
2. Where do existing methods fail or underperform?
3. What methodological weakness justifies your proposed approach?

The research gap must be clearly articulated and logically connected to the problem defined in Assignment 1. If no genuine gap can be identified, the problem statement must be reconsidered.

### E. Positioning of the Thesis Contribution

Conclude the review with a short section explaining:

* How your thesis differs from or extends existing approaches
* What specific comparative dimension you will investigate
* Which baseline methods are justified based on the literature

This section bridges Assignment 2 and Assignment 3 (experimental design).

## 3. Academic Expectations for an Engineering Thesis

At the engineering level, the literature review must demonstrate:

* Understanding of dominant methodological approaches
* Ability to critically assess empirical results
* Awareness of reproducibility and evaluation standards
* Structured synthesis rather than descriptive reporting

The review must not:

* Be a sequence of paper summaries
* Avoid critique
* Overclaim novelty

An engineering thesis is expected to show informed positioning, not theoretical innovation at a doctoral level.

## 4. Quality Evaluation Criteria

Assignment 2 will be evaluated based on:

1. Relevance and quality of selected sources
2. Analytical depth (comparison vs. summary)
3. Logical coherence between identified gap and research problem
4. Clarity of methodological synthesis
5. Academic writing quality and citation standards

Approval of this assignment confirms that the thesis topic is:

* Scientifically grounded
* Methodologically justified
* Ready for experimental planning

## 5. Position Within the Seminar Continuum

* Assignment 1 defined the problem.
* Assignment 2 validates and positions it.

The outcome of this stage determines:

* Choice of baseline models
* Evaluation metrics
* Experimental comparisons
* Design constraints

Assignment 3 will transform this theoretical positioning into a concrete experimental architecture.

A superficial literature review results in a poorly designed experiment. Therefore, this stage is methodological, not formal.
