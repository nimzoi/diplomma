# Assignment 9 — Results Analysis and Interpretation

## 1. Objective of the Assignment

The purpose of this assignment is to transform experimental outputs into scientifically structured conclusions.

At this stage, the student must demonstrate:

* Ability to synthesize results across experiments
* Critical reasoning beyond metric comparison
* Awareness of model limitations and failure modes
* Capacity to interpret model behavior in relation to the research question

An engineering thesis is not evaluated on numerical results alone, but on the quality of interpretation and analytical maturity. This assignment marks the beginning of the formal scientific articulation of the practical contribution.

## 2. Scope of Work to Be Delivered

The student must submit:

* A structured analytical report (4–6 pages)
* Consolidated result tables
* Error analysis
* Interpretability analysis
* Explicit linkage to the research hypothesis

The document must contain the following components:

### A. Consolidated Comparative Results

Provide a unified comparison including:

* All baseline models
* The primary model
* Key metric values
* Variability measures (if cross-validation was used)

The presentation must:

* Maintain metric consistency
* Avoid selective highlighting
* Clearly indicate statistically or practically meaningful differences

The student must demonstrate an ability to see the full experimental landscape rather than isolated results.

### B. Structured Error Analysis

This section is essential.

The student must analyze:

* Where the model fails
* Types of misclassifications or high-error predictions
* Patterns in incorrect outputs
* Whether specific classes or ranges are systematically problematic

If applicable, include:

* Confusion matrix analysis
* Residual distribution analysis
* Subgroup performance comparison

The objective is to identify structural weaknesses rather than isolated mistakes. Error analysis must contribute to understanding model behavior, not merely report statistics.

### C. Model Interpretability and Feature Contribution

Depending on the model type, implement and analyze:

* Feature importance measures
* SHAP values or equivalent interpretability methods
* Sensitivity analysis

The student must answer:

* Which features drive predictions?
* Are these findings consistent with domain expectations?
* Do they reveal potential bias or overfitting?

Interpretability must not be decorative; it must support analytical reasoning.

### D. Robustness and Stability Assessment

The student must critically evaluate:

* Performance variance across folds
* Sensitivity to hyperparameter changes
* Stability under small perturbations (if applicable)

Discuss:

* Whether results are robust
* Whether improvements over baseline are consistent
* Whether conclusions are reliable

Engineering rigor requires cautious interpretation of marginal gains.

### E. Alignment with Research Question and Hypothesis

This section reconnects the analysis to Assignment 1.

The student must explicitly state:

* Whether the hypothesis is supported or rejected
* Whether the research question has been empirically answered
* What evidence justifies the conclusion

Conclusions must be evidence-driven and proportional to the results obtained. Overgeneralization is unacceptable.

## 3. Academic Expectations for an Engineering Thesis

At the engineering level, analytical synthesis must demonstrate:

* Structured reasoning
* Critical evaluation of strengths and weaknesses
* Honest assessment of limitations
* Logical coherence

The thesis is expected to:

* Provide insight into model behavior
* Justify claims with empirical evidence
* Distinguish between statistical improvement and practical significance

It must avoid:

* Metric-only comparison
* Overinterpretation of minor differences
* Ignoring negative findings

Recognizing limitations strengthens, rather than weakens, the thesis.

## 4. Quality Evaluation Criteria

Assignment 9 will be evaluated based on:

1. Depth of comparative analysis
2. Quality of error interpretation
3. Meaningful use of interpretability tools
4. Logical connection to research question
5. Critical and balanced conclusions

Approval of this assignment confirms that the experimental phase has been scientifically synthesized.

## 5. Position Within the Seminar Continuum

* Assignment 8 finalized model experimentation.
* Assignment 9 translates results into structured scientific insight.
* Assignments 10–12 will focus on formal writing of methodological chapters, structural coherence of the thesis document, and finalization of conclusions and limitations.

At this stage:

* No major experimental redesign should occur.
* Interpretation must precede writing formal chapters.
* All empirical claims must be defensible and reproducible.

The thesis now moves from engineering execution to academic articulation.
